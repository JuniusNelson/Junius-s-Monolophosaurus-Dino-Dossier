Monolophosarus

Who and where were the fossils founded?

In 1981, Dong Zhiming, a Chinese paleontologist,made the discovery of a nearly complete skeleton belonging to a previously unknown theropod during strati-graphic exploration conducted for the oil industry. The fossil, however, remained buried until it was officially unearthed in 1984.

genus

Monolophosaurus is a genus of theropod dinosaur that lived in the Middle Jurassic period, approximately 165 million years ago. Fossils of Monolophosaurus have been found in China, and it is characterized by a distinctive crest on its snout, which gives it its name ("monolopho" means "single crest"). This dinosaur was likely a carnivorous predator, and its fossils contribute to our understanding of theropod evolution during the Jurassic period.
